Submitting Your Idea
to the AI Agents Hackathon 2025

A comprehensive guide to successfully navigating the submission process

Deadline

April 30, 2025

11:59 PM PST

Portal

aka.ms/agenthack

Introduction

This report provides a comprehensive, step-by-step guide for submitting a project to the AI Agents Hackathon 2025, a virtual event hosted by Microsoft. The submission process is centralized through a dedicated online portal, and participants are required to provide detailed information about their project, including its concept, the problem it addresses, the technology used, and their team's experience.

This guide is structured to walk participants through each stage of the submission, from accessing the portal to finalizing their entry, ensuring all requirements are met for a successful submission before the April 30, 2025 deadline [379].

1

Accessing the Submission Portal

The initial and most critical step in the submission process is to correctly locate and access the official hackathon portal. This centralized platform serves as the gateway for all submission-related activities, including team registration, project details entry, and file uploads.

Locating the Official Submission Dashboard

Critical Information

The official submission portal for the AI Agents Hackathon 2025 is located at:
https://aka.ms/agenthack [391] [418]

This link is consistently referenced across all official Microsoft documentation and promotional materials for the event, including the main event page and the official rules [379] [391]. Participants should navigate to this URL to begin the submission process.

The portal is the designated platform where all project entries must be submitted to be considered for the competition. It is crucial to use this specific link, as it directs to the official Microsoft-managed platform, ensuring that the submission is received and processed correctly.

Logging In to Your Account

Authentication Process

Upon navigating to the official submission portal, participants will be required to log in to their account. The login process is a standard authentication step designed to verify the identity of the user and associate the submission with the correct team and individual profiles.

It is essential to use the same credentials that were used during the initial registration for the hackathon to ensure continuity and avoid any discrepancies. If a participant has not yet registered for the event, they will need to do so first before they can access the submission dashboard [391].

Navigating to the Submissions Section

After successfully logging in to the hackathon dashboard, the next step is to locate and navigate to the "Submissions" section. This section is the core of the submission process, where participants will find the official submission form and upload their project materials.

Navigation Tips

  • Look for "Submissions" in the main navigation bar
  • Check for dedicated tiles on the dashboard homepage
  • The section is prominently displayed for easy access
2

It is designed to capture all the essential elements of the AI agent, from its core concept to the technical implementation. It is crucial to fill out every field in the submission form to ensure that the entry is complete and eligible for judging [414].

Selecting a Theme

The first step in the submission form is to select a theme for the project. The AI Agents Hackathon 2025 features several distinct categories, each with its own focus and prize [379] [388].

Important Note

Each team can only win in one category, so it is important to choose the one that best represents the project's strengths [379].

Describing Your Idea

Character Limit: 2000 characters

The "Idea Description" section requires a clear and concise explanation of your project, showcasing creativity and innovation while providing judges with a high-level understanding of your AI agent.

When crafting the idea description, it is important to start with a strong opening statement that clearly articulates the core concept of the AI agent. This should be followed by a more detailed explanation of the agent's functionality, including its key features and capabilities.

Do Include:
  • • Strong opening statement
  • • Key features and capabilities
  • • Unique value proposition
  • • Accessible language
Avoid:
  • • Overly technical jargon
  • • Vague descriptions
  • • Exceeding character limit
  • • Typos and errors

Explaining the Problem

The "Problem Statement" section is where participants define the real-world problem their AI agent is designed to solve. This is a critical component that provides context and justification for the project.

Key Elements of a Strong Problem Statement:

Problem Definition
  • • Specific and focused
  • • Clear pain points
  • • Evidence-supported
  • • Relatable context
Impact Demonstration
  • • Scale and scope
  • • Quantified metrics
  • • Real-world examples
  • • Urgency and importance

Listing Your Technology Stack

The "Technology Stack" section requires listing all technologies, frameworks, and tools used to build the AI agent. This demonstrates technical expertise and provides judges with insight into the project's implementation.

Technology Stack Organization

The "Hackathon Experience" section allows participants to share their background and experience, providing judges with a holistic view of the team and adding a human element to the submission.

First-time Participants

Share excitement, motivation, and fresh perspective

Experienced Teams

3

Uploading Your Submission Materials

In addition to filling out the submission form, participants must upload supporting materials that provide detailed information about their project. These materials are essential for judges to gain a comprehensive understanding of the project's concept, design, and implementation.

Preparing Your Idea Submission Deck

Format Requirement: PDF Only

The submission guidelines specify that the idea submission deck must be in PDF format to ensure compatibility across platforms.

Recommended Deck Structure

1 Title Slide (Project Title, Team Name, Members)
2 Problem Statement
3 Solution Overview
4 Technology Stack
5 Demo (Screenshots/Video)
6 Impact and Future Work
7 Team Introduction

Uploading the PDF File

Upload Process

  1. Locate the file upload section at the end of the submission form
  2. Click "Upload File" or "Choose File" button
  3. Select your PDF file in the file browser
  4. Click "Upload" or "Submit" to attach the file
  5. Verify the upload was successful through confirmation message

After selecting the PDF file, attach it to the submission form and verify the upload was successful. The portal will typically display a confirmation message or preview of the uploaded file. Double-check the file name and size to ensure the correct file was uploaded.

4

Finalizing and Submitting Your Idea

The final stage involves a thorough review of all information and materials, followed by official confirmation of your submission. This is your last opportunity to make changes before your project is locked in for judging.

Reviewing All Entered Information

Comprehensive Review Checklist

Form Sections
  • • Project description accuracy
  • • Problem statement clarity
  • • Technology stack completeness
  • • Team member details
Materials & Details
  • • Correct theme selection
  • • Uploaded PDF verification
  • • File accessibility check
  • • Fresh pair of eyes review

Before finalizing your submission, conduct a comprehensive review of all entered information. This is your last chance to catch errors, omissions, or inconsistencies. It is a good practice to have a teammate or trusted colleague review your submission as well.

Confirming the Submission of Your Project

Final Confirmation

Once you have completed your review and are satisfied with all information, click the "Submit" or "Confirm Submission" button.

Note: After confirmation, no further changes can be made.

After confirming your submission, you should receive a confirmation message or email from the hackathon organizers, acknowledging that your project has been successfully submitted. Keep a record of this confirmation for your records.

Acknowledging the Submission Deadline

April 30, 2025, at 11:59 PM PST [379]

Any submissions received after this deadline will not be considered.

Submit Early

Do not wait until the last minute to avoid technical difficulties from high traffic volumes.

Plan Accordingly

Complete and submit your project well in advance to allow ample time for review and corrections.
